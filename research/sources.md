## Research Areas

| Research area                                             | Core question                                                                                                                       | What it covers                                                                                                                                                     | Boundary                                                                                              |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Commercial Fit, ICP, and Problem Context**           | What commercially meaningful problem is being solved, for which type of company, and when is outbound a sensible motion?            | Problem severity and maturity; ICP hypotheses; commercial fit; offer fit; the relationship between ACV, sales cycle, buyer environment, and sales motion.          | Does not select specific accounts or map individual stakeholders.                                     |
| **2. Target Account Prioritisation and Account Research** | Given a plausible ICP, which specific companies deserve attention first?                                                            | Account-fit criteria; account context; tiering; prioritisation; expected value; research depth.                                                                    | Does not determine why now or identify the people inside an account.                                  |
| **3. Stakeholder Mapping and Buying-Committee Strategy**  | Inside a selected account, who should be engaged, in what order, and why?                                                           | Entry points; users, champions, economic buyers, and blockers; multi-threading; role-specific interests and risks.                                                 | Does not define the ICP or find and verify contact details.                                           |
| **4. Contact Data, Enrichment, and Verification**         | How are selected stakeholders turned into reliable, usable contact records?                                                         | Data sources; enrichment; identity resolution; freshness; deduplication; source conflicts; email verification and its limits.                                      | Does not judge account fit, buying intent, or sender reputation.                                      |
| **5. Timing and Trigger Signals**                         | Why should this account be contacted now rather than later?                                                                         | Signal interpretation; trigger-to-problem hypotheses; timing windows; false positives; priority changes.                                                           | Does not establish baseline account fit or construct the outreach message.                            |
| **6. Deliverability and Sender Infrastructure**           | Can outreach reach inboxes sustainably without damaging sender health?                                                              | Sending identity; SPF, DKIM, and DMARC; domains and mailboxes; volume controls; reputation; bounces; spam complaints; unsubscribes.                                | Does not validate whether a contact is the right person or whether the message is relevant.           |
| **7. Messaging and Conversation Entry**                   | How is known account and timing context translated into an honest reason to start a conversation?                                   | Subject lines; opening lines; business relevance; proof; tone; CTA; message structure; persona-specific framing.                                                   | Does not define product-market fit, select accounts, or determine the wider follow-up plan.           |
| **8. Cadence and Channel Orchestration**                  | How should first touches, follow-ups, calls, and LinkedIn interactions work together over time?                                     | Channel roles; sequence design; touch order; spacing; persistence; stopping rules; breakup or close-the-loop messages.                                             | Does not cover detailed call execution or what happens after a prospect responds.                     |
| **9. Live Conversation and Objection Handling**           | When a prospect engages live, especially by phone, how should the seller open, handle resistance, and create a useful conversation? | Call openers; permission-based approaches; initial discovery; reflex resistance versus substantive objections; live follow-up language.                            | Does not set qualification criteria, define sequence strategy, or determine account selection.        |
| **10. Reply Handling, Qualification, and Handoff**        | Once a prospect responds, how should that interaction be interpreted, progressed, qualified, or handed to the right owner?          | Reply classification; referrals; out-of-office responses; objections; unsubscribe handling; qualification; routing; handoff; movement from meeting to opportunity. | Does not teach cold-call mechanics or determine upstream ICP, targeting, timing, or message strategy. |

# Collect Public Expert Material

## Purpose

Build a compact, evidence-backed source library for assigned experts.

The goal is not to collect everything they have published. The goal is to capture the most relevant and usable material for the research areas defined in `research/sources.md`.

## Required reading

Before starting:

1. Read all files in `.agents/rules/`.
2. Read `research/sources.md`.
3. Read the active task plan.
4. Confirm target experts, source quantity, source types, recency standard, and output folders.

## Source selection

Prefer sources that are:

* original material by the expert
* recent under the project standard
* clearly related to a defined research area
* operational rather than motivational
* specific enough to support a later research insight

Prioritize:

1. Original LinkedIn posts
2. Original YouTube videos, webinars, or podcast appearances
3. Personal newsletter or company blog
4. First-party case studies
5. Credible interviews

Avoid collecting:

* generic personal-brand content
* repeated versions of the same framework
* promotional announcements without substance
* general sales advice with no outbound application
* content with no identifiable operational claim

## Collection workflow

### 1. Select source

Check that the source is relevant to an assigned expert and a defined research area.

### 2. Capture metadata

Create one evidence file per source using the standard source-record format.

### 3. Extract only what is needed

For each source, capture:

* the specific claim, framework, or technique
* the exact supporting excerpt or timestamp
* the relevant research area
* a concise original summary
* limits or caveats

For a video or podcast, identify the exact timestamp before writing a summary.

### 4. Preserve limitations

State when:

* the source is undated
* the full content was inaccessible
* a claim is presented without evidence
* the material is a personal opinion rather than demonstrated practice
* the idea overlaps substantially with an existing source

### 5. Stop at the task boundary

Do not collect extra sources once the task-plan requirement is complete.

Quality beats volume.

## Quality gate

Before marking a source complete, verify:

* It belongs to an assigned expert.
* It maps to a defined research area.
* It includes a canonical URL.
* Its date status is recorded honestly.
* Its excerpt or timestamp supports the written note.
* It does not copy full copyrighted third-party content.
* It adds value beyond material already captured.

## Final response

Use the repository’s standard completion report.
